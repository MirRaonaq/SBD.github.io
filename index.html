Abstract

In this project, we will be focusing on sentence boundary detection (SBD) without punctuation marks. Researching current solutions from elephant will provide us with their results. With the information gathered from the current data, I will be creating my own approach of SBD for the English and Arabic languages.

Previous Solutions

Elephant currently has models for sentence and word boundary detection of English, Dutch and Italian. SBD, however is never perfect for any language. As for English, there are many imperfections of SBD. Punctuation marks like a period can be used for abbreviations. For example, the abbreviation "U.S." in the English language is short for United States. The periods used does not classify as the end of a sentence. There are many programs out there that try to combat this situation but none have come out to perfectly be accurate. As for the Arabic language, there are not much research done on SBD. Arabic is complex in terms of sentence boundary. Most of the research done have been speech related experiments.
